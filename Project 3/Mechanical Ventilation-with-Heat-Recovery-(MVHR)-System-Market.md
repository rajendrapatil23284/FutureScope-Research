Definition:

Mechanical Ventilation with Heat Recovery (MVHR) systems are energy-efficient ventilation systems that simultaneously remove stale air from a building and recover heat from the extracted air to warm incoming fresh air, improving indoor air quality and energy efficiency.

Market Dynamics:

The increasing focus on energy efficiency and sustainability in buildings is driving the demand for MVHR systems.

Growing awareness about indoor air quality and health concerns is also contributing to market growth.

Technological advancements in heat recovery systems, such as improved filtration and control features, are enhancing the efficiency of MVHR systems.

Market Trends:

Integration of smart controls and automation technologies in MVHR systems for enhanced performance and monitoring.

Rising popularity of modular and compact MVHR units for easier installation and maintenance.

Increasing adoption of heat pump MVHR systems for greater energy savings and carbon footprint reduction.
